# Data Flywheel Map — SuccessOS AI

> Score each loop 1-5. Your weakest loop is where competitors attack first.
> Scored at ground zero. The first flywheel is correction data, not network effects.

## Flywheel Loops

| Loop | What It Measures | Score 1 | Score 5 | Score |
|------|------------------|---------|---------|-------|
| **Correction** | Do users fix AI outputs? Is that signal captured and reused? | No capture | Automated retraining | 1/5 |
| **Preference** | Does the product learn individual/team preferences over time? | Stateless | Deep personalization | 1/5 |
| **Domain Context** | Does usage in one area improve quality in adjacent areas? | Siloed | Cross-domain transfer | 2/5 |
| **Network** | Does each new user/team make it better for everyone? | Isolated | Strong network effects | 1/5 |

### Correction Loop — 1/5
**What you capture today:** Approve/Dismiss exists but nothing is stored.
**How it compounds:** Every override should become training data tied to account context and eventual outcome.

### Preference Loop — 1/5
**What you capture today:** No memory of team behavior. Every session starts from zero.
**How it compounds:** The product can learn preferred actions, escalation patterns, and dashboard defaults by CSM/team.

### Domain Context Loop — 2/5
**What you capture today:** Shared account data exists, but relationships are rule-based rather than learned.
**How it compounds:** Meeting notes, support patterns, usage shifts, and renewal outcomes should improve each other over time.

### Network Loop — 1/5
**What you capture today:** No cross-customer learning today.
**How it compounds:** Future path is anonymized benchmarks and intervention outcome patterns.

**Total Flywheel Score: 5/20**
**Weakest Loop:** Correction.
**Fix for weakest loop:** Persist approve/dismiss/edit actions with account context, recommendation, decision, timestamp, and eventual outcome.

## 90-Day Encroachment Plan

*Partner attack plan: Salesforce ships native churn insights inside the CRM.*

**Attacker:** Salesforce / Einstein

**Attack vector (target the weakest loop):** Use existing CRM data and workflow distribution to make CS intelligence feel like a native feature, not a new procurement decision.

**Weeks 1-4 — what they ship:** Account health summary, churn-risk alert, and renewal-risk explanation inside the Salesforce account page.

**Weeks 5-8 — how they poach users:** Bundle it into existing Salesforce contracts and position standalone CS AI as unnecessary integration overhead.

**Weeks 9-12 — why users don't come back:** The insights are good enough, live where the CSM already works, and require no separate onboarding.

**Your defense:** Salesforce optimizes CRM workflows. SuccessOS optimizes customer outcome workflows. The moat is not health scoring; it is intervention prioritization, human approval, cross-system intelligence, and accumulated correction data.
