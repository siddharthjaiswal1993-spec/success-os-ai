## What's Exemplary

**The Meeting Intelligence workflow.** Paste meeting notes → structured churn/expansion analysis + follow-up email is the highest-value, lowest-friction AI workflow in CS. It requires no integration, produces immediate value, and works on the most common CS work product (meeting notes). This is the right landing wedge.

**The signal-to-action loop design.** The 6-stage loop (Ingest → Classify → Score → Recommend → Act → Verify) and the clear boundary between AI-autonomous and human-approved stages is a principled governance design. The boundary rule (internal state = AI-autonomous, customer-facing = human-required) is specific, defensible, and consistent.

**The approval workflow as a feedback mechanism.** Designing the approve/dismiss UI to capture structured rejection reasons — not just record the decision — is a product insight that most AI products miss. Every dismissal with a reason is a negative training example. That data compounds over time.

**The vulnerability scoring honesty.** Scoring the data flywheel at 5/20 (ground zero) and documenting the kill criteria is exactly the level of intellectual honesty that good product strategy requires. Most AI product decks claim moat without evidence. This one scores it honestly and explains what needs to be built.

## What Would Come Next

**The Margin framework completion.** The cost model and pricing strategy (The Margin) needs the same rigour as The Bet and The Moat. The cost-per-seat model and gross margin analysis at scale are critical for the business strategy.

**CRM and CSP integration specs.** The product value depends on connecting to Salesforce/HubSpot (account data), Gong/Chorus (call recordings), and product analytics tools. The integration architecture — which data comes from where, freshness model, normalisation approach — needs specification.

**Cross-customer pattern intelligence.** The churn prediction improves when trained across multiple customers. Designing the privacy model, consent framework, and data minimisation approach for cross-customer learning is both a technical and product governance problem.
