# Glossary — SuccessOS AI

Definitions for terms used throughout the SuccessOS AI documentation.

---

## Product Terms

**Account Health Score**  
A composite 0–100 score representing the overall health of a customer account. Computed from multiple signal inputs including meeting sentiment, product adoption, support ticket volume, and engagement trends. Color-coded: ≥75 Green, 55–74 Yellow, 40–54 Orange, <40 Red.

**Agent Action**  
A recommended action generated by an AI agent. Each action has a type, priority, rationale, confidence score, and classification (Internal or External). External actions require human approval before execution.

**Agentic Workflow**  
An automated AI workflow that triggers on defined signals, processes account context, and produces outputs (risk flags, recommendations, drafts). Workflows operate at defined autonomy levels (L1–L3).

**Autonomy Level**  
The degree to which an agentic workflow can execute without human intervention. L1 = Generate only; L2 = Recommend + route; L3 = Execute internally; L4 = Full automation (not implemented).

**Churn Risk Delta**  
The directional change in churn risk based on a specific signal or meeting. Values: Increased, Decreased, Stable.

**Confidence Score**  
A 0.0–1.0 probability estimate representing the AI's confidence in a classification or recommendation. Displayed as High (≥0.80), Medium (0.60–0.79), Low (<0.60).

**CS Director**  
The manager of a Customer Success team. Primary decision-maker for CS tools and processes. Owns the churn and NRR targets.

**CSM (Customer Success Manager)**  
The individual responsible for managing day-to-day customer relationships and outcomes. Primary end user of SuccessOS AI.

**Customer Memory Model**  
The structured, longitudinal data store for each account. Contains: Account Profile, Health State, Activity Log, Known Risks, Expansion Signals, Prior Recommendations.

**Expansion Signal**  
A detected indication that a customer may be interested in expanding their contract — additional seats, new locations, new modules, or new use cases.

**External Action**  
Any agent-recommended action that would be visible to or received by a customer. Requires explicit human approval before execution.

**Feature Impact Agent**  
An agentic workflow that takes a product feature description as input and identifies which customer accounts are most relevant based on account context and tags.

**Health Score Delta (30d)**  
The change in account health score over the past 30 days. Positive = improving, Negative = declining.

**Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)**  
The design principle that certain AI outputs require human review and approval before being executed. In SuccessOS AI, all External actions are HITL-gated.

**Internal Action**  
An agent-recommended action that affects only internal system state — updating a health score, creating a task, logging a signal. Can be auto-executed without human approval.

**Meeting Intelligence**  
The AI workflow that processes submitted meeting notes to detect churn risk signals, expansion signals, and key themes, and generates a structured analysis with recommended actions.

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention)**  
A metric that measures revenue retained and expanded from existing customers, accounting for churn, expansion, and contraction. Formula: (Beginning ARR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Beginning ARR.

**QBR (Quarterly Business Review)**  
A structured customer-facing review document covering the past quarter's performance, current health, risks, opportunities, and recommended next steps. SuccessOS AI generates QBR drafts for CSM review.

**Risk Flag**  
A logged signal indicating a specific risk for an account, with severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low), description, and detected date.

**Signal**  
Any data point that informs account health. Includes: meeting note signals (language-based), product usage signals (structured data), support ticket signals, engagement signals.

**Signal-to-Action Loop**  
The end-to-end flow from signal ingestion → AI interpretation → recommendation → human approval → execution → outcome capture. The core operating model of SuccessOS AI.

**Triggering Phrase**  
A specific quote from a meeting note that the AI identified as a churn risk or expansion signal. Shown alongside the analysis to allow CSM to verify.

## Technical Terms

**Context Assembly**  
The process of gathering and structuring account data (profile, health history, meeting history, signals) into a prompt context for LLM inference.

**Grounded Generation**  
A prompting technique that restricts the LLM to only make claims supported by information explicitly provided in the prompt context, reducing hallucination risk.

**LLM (Large Language Model)**  
A deep learning model trained on large text corpora that can understand and generate natural language. SuccessOS AI uses Claude (Anthropic) as its primary LLM.

**Prompt Template**  
A structured prompt with variable placeholders that is populated with account context and submitted to the LLM to generate a specific output type.

**Schema Validation**  
Post-generation validation that AI outputs conform to a predefined structure (required fields, valid value ranges, valid enum values). Used to catch malformed or incomplete outputs before display.

**Tenant Isolation**  
The architectural guarantee that data from one customer organization is never exposed to another organization in the same multi-tenant deployment.

## Eval Terms

**F1 Score**  
The harmonic mean of precision and recall. Used to evaluate classification accuracy where both false positives and false negatives are important. Formula: F1 = 2 × (Precision × Recall) / (Precision + Recall).

**Offline Eval**  
Evaluation run against a fixed labeled dataset before deployment. Tests that model/prompt changes maintain quality above defined thresholds.

**Online Eval**  
Continuous measurement of AI quality in production, based on user behavior (acceptance rate, dismissal reasons) and outcome tracking.

**Precision**  
The fraction of positive predictions that are correct. Precision = TP / (TP + FP).

**Precision@K**  
The fraction of the top-K results that are correct. Used for ranking problems like feature impact matching. Precision@3 = (correct results in top 3) / 3.

**Recall**  
The fraction of actual positives that were correctly identified. Recall = TP / (TP + FN).

**Red-Teaming**  
Structured adversarial testing where a team deliberately attempts to break the AI system — finding failure modes, edge cases, and safety boundary violations.
