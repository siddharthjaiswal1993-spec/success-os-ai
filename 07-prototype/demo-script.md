# Demo Script — SuccessOS AI

A guided walkthrough for portfolio presentation or user testing. Two versions: 3-minute quick demo and 8-minute full demo.

---

## 3-Minute Demo (Highlight Reel)

**Opening line:**
"SuccessOS AI is a Customer Success intelligence platform. The core problem it solves: CS teams have more signals than they can process, and by the time they act, it's often too late. This is what AI-first signal-to-action looks like."

**Step 1 — Dashboard (30 seconds)**
> Land on the dashboard

"Here's a CS Director's view — 8 accounts, health scores at a glance. CloudKitchen Pro and FranchiseWorks are red — renewal coming up in 30 and 65 days. Two accounts that need attention this week."

> Click on CloudKitchen Pro

---

**Step 2 — Account Detail: Signals (45 seconds)**
> Click the Timeline tab

"The timeline shows every meeting note submitted by the CSM, with AI-detected signals. This meeting from last week triggered a churn risk flag — the AI pulled out the phrase 'we're not sure this is the right fit' and flagged it as High confidence. The CSM didn't have to write this analysis — they submitted the meeting notes and the platform did the interpretation."

---

**Step 3 — Recommended Action with Approval (45 seconds)**
> Click the Actions tab

"The platform recommended an Executive Check-in — here's the full reasoning. I can see the signal that triggered it, the confidence level, and a draft communication. I haven't seen this yet so I'll review it — it looks right. [Click Approve]. Done. The action is logged, my intervention is on record, and the AI didn't send anything to the customer without my approval."

---

**Step 4 — QBR Generation (30 seconds)**
> Click QBR tab, click Generate QBR

"QBR prep was one of the most hated parts of being a CSM — hours of pulling data, writing summaries, formatting slides. [QBR appears] This is a full account-aware QBR, generated in seconds. It pulls in the health score trend, the open risk items, and the expansion conversations from this account's history. I'd review it, make any edits, and it's ready. What used to take 4 hours takes 5 minutes."

---

**Closing line:**
"The principle behind the product: AI does the pattern recognition and the first draft. The CSM does the judgment and the approval. Signal-to-action, with the human in the loop."

---

## 8-Minute Full Demo

### Part 1: CS Director Overview (2 minutes)

**Dashboard tour:**
"As a CS Director, my job is knowing which accounts need attention before they tell me. On this dashboard, I can see the full portfolio health — 67 average health score, two accounts in the red zone, $500K of ARR renewing in the next 60 days. The old way to get this view was a weekly status spreadsheet that CSMs filled out manually. This is live."

> Filter to "At Risk"

"I can narrow to just the at-risk accounts. CloudKitchen Pro: health 44, renewal in 30 days, three open risk flags. FranchiseWorks: health 48, renewal in 65 days. These are the two accounts I'm going to focus on."

---

### Part 2: Account Deep Dive — CloudKitchen Pro (2.5 minutes)

> Click CloudKitchen Pro → Overview tab

"ARR is $145K. Renewal in 30 days. Health score is 44 and trending down — this is a customer who was healthy 6 weeks ago and has deteriorated fast."

> Click Timeline tab

"Let me see what happened. Two weeks ago, the CSM had a check-in call. They submitted their notes here. The platform analyzed the notes and flagged a churn risk increase — supporting quote: 'we haven't seen the ROI we expected.' One week ago, second meeting — the platform flagged another churn signal: 'our team is barely using it.' These notes were just sitting in the CRM before — no one was reading them systematically."

> Click Actions tab

"The platform recommended three actions. First: Executive Check-in — critical priority, renewal in 30 days. Second: Adoption Recovery — product usage data shows only 2 of 8 modules actively used. Third: Proposal to extend the trial period on one module as a retention offer. Each recommendation has a rationale. I can approve, edit, or dismiss."

> Approve the first action

"I'll approve the Executive Check-in. This is now queued. The draft message is ready for the CSM to send after reviewing it."

---

### Part 3: Meeting Intelligence (1.5 minutes)

> Navigate back to Dashboard → Click FranchiseWorks → Timeline tab

"FranchiseWorks is interesting — they have a risk flag AND an expansion signal from the same meeting. Look at this note from last week: the CSM flagged concern about franchise adoption inconsistency, AND the customer asked about rolling out to 3 more franchise regions."

> Show the signal cards side by side

"The platform separated these into two signals: a churn risk around adoption (which needs attention now) and an expansion signal around regional growth (which is an opportunity). It's not 'this account is good' or 'this account is bad' — it's the specific, contextualized intelligence that helps the CSM have a smarter conversation."

---

### Part 4: Feature Impact Agent (1.5 minutes)

> Navigate to Feature Impact

"Now I'm the Product team. We just shipped a multi-location benchmarking dashboard. I want to know which customers should hear about this. I type in the feature description..."

> Type "Multi-location benchmarking dashboard — compare performance across all locations"

> Click Analyze

"In seconds: FranchiseWorks is High relevance — 18 franchise locations, this feature is directly relevant. UrbanFit Studios: High — multi-location fitness chain. CloudKitchen Pro: Medium — multiple kitchen sites. The reasoning is shown for each match. My CSMs now have a prioritized outreach list for this feature launch. Before this, we'd send a product update email to everyone and hope someone clicked."

---

### Part 5: QBR Generation (30 seconds)

> Go to NovaHealth → QBR tab

"Last piece. NovaHealth is a healthy account renewing in 140 days. It's QBR season. Instead of the CSM spending 4 hours pulling data..."

> Click Generate QBR

"[QBR appears] Full account-aware QBR in 8 seconds. Executive summary, health trend, expansion opportunities — the 3-site expansion that came up in the last meeting is in here. The CSM reviews it, makes their edits, and they own the final output. The AI generated the first draft; the CSM owns the relationship."

---

### Closing (30 seconds)

"Three things this demo shows:

One: The signal-to-action loop. From meeting notes to recommendation to approved action — all in one place.

Two: Human-in-the-loop by design. Every customer-facing action requires approval. The AI operates as an intelligent assistant, not an autonomous agent.

Three: CS intelligence that compounds. Every note submitted, every signal labeled, every action outcome captured — the platform gets smarter about which signals matter and which recommendations get acted on."
