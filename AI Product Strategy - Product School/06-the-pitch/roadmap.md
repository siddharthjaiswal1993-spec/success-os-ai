## Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):**

SuccessOS AI helps Customer Success teams act before churn becomes obvious by turning scattered customer signals into prioritized workflows.

**The case:**
1. Why now: CS teams already have signals across CRM, product usage, support, and meetings, but they are hard to act on together.
2. What's defensible: Workflow depth, human approval, correction data, and learning from outcomes.
3. The economics: Seat-based pricing fits how CS teams buy software, and AI cost stays manageable if model routing is designed well.

**The risks:**
1. Trust / failure modes: CSMs ignore recommendations if outputs are wrong or unclear.
2. Scale / governance: Weak audit logs, hallucinations, or missing approvals reduce enterprise trust.
3. Competitive: Salesforce, Gainsight, or Gong add similar insight layers inside tools customers already use.

**The ask:**

Prioritize integrations, correction-data infrastructure, and outcome tracking before adding more AI features. The flywheel matters more than model sophistication.

## M1 Baseline vs. Now
*Your 3-sentence AI strategy from Module 1 vs. what you'd say now:*

**M1 baseline:**

Build an AI-powered customer success assistant that analyzes account data, identifies risk, and recommends actions.

**Now:**

This should not just be an assistant. It should become a customer outcome operating system for CS teams. The important asset is not the model itself, but the correction and intervention data created through trusted workflows. Success depends on becoming the fastest way for a CS team to detect, prioritize, and act on churn risk before it is obvious.
