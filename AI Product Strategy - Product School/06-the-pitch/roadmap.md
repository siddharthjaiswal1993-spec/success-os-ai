## Board Pitch

**Thesis (1 sentence):**

SuccessOS AI helps Customer Success teams intervene before churn becomes visible by turning fragmented customer signals into prioritized, actionable workflows.

**The case:**
1. Why now: CS teams are overwhelmed by signals across CRM, product analytics, support systems, and meetings. LLMs make it possible to convert those signals into actions instead of dashboards.
2. What's defensible: Human-in-the-loop workflows, cross-system intelligence, correction data, and outcome learning. Competitors can copy summaries faster than they can copy operational trust and intervention history.
3. The economics: Seat-based pricing aligns with how CS teams buy software. AI cost is a small fraction of revenue, enabling >90% gross margins at scale.

**The risks:**
1. Trust / failure modes: CSMs ignore recommendations or lose confidence after incorrect outputs.
2. Scale / governance: Poor auditability, hallucinations, or weak approval controls reduce enterprise adoption.
3. Competitive: Salesforce, Gainsight, or Gong embed similar capabilities into existing workflows before we establish correction-data advantages.

**The ask:**

Invest in integrations, correction-data infrastructure, and outcome tracking before expanding AI capabilities. The flywheel matters more than model sophistication.

## M1 Baseline vs. Now
*Your 3-sentence AI strategy from Module 1 vs. what you'd say now:*

**M1 baseline:**

Build an AI-powered customer success assistant that analyzes account data, identifies risk, and recommends actions.

**Now:**

The product is not an assistant; it is a customer outcome operating system. The key asset is not the model, but the correction and intervention data generated through trusted workflows. Success depends on becoming the fastest way for a CS team to detect, prioritize, and act on churn risk before it becomes obvious.
