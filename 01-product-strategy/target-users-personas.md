# Target Users and Personas

---

## Primary Users

### Persona 1: Maya — Customer Success Manager

**Role:** Individual contributor CSM at a Series C enterprise SaaS company  
**Accounts managed:** 28 enterprise accounts, $4.2M ARR total book of business  
**Tools used daily:** Salesforce, Gainsight (or equivalent), Gong, Zendesk, Slack, Google Docs  
**Years in CS:** 4

**Goals:**
- Avoid churn surprises — know about risks before her manager does
- Spend more time on strategic account conversations, less on documentation
- Produce QBR materials that actually impress executive stakeholders
- Surface expansion signals before her AM counterpart does

**Pain points:**
- Meeting notes pile up; she never re-reads them for patterns
- Health score in Gainsight reflects her last manual update, not reality
- QBR prep takes 4+ hours per account; she does 8 per quarter
- She finds out about product issues through customers, not proactively

**JTBD (Jobs to Be Done):**
- Help me understand which accounts are quietly at risk
- Help me know what to do about them before it's too late
- Help me prepare for conversations and executive briefings faster
- Help me demonstrate value to my CS leadership team

**Adoption risk:** Maya will adopt quickly if the Meeting Intelligence output is better than what she produces manually. She will churn from the product if AI outputs feel generic or uncalibrated to her account context.

### Persona 2: Raj — Director of Customer Success

**Role:** CS leader managing a team of 8 CSMs, $35M ARR book  
**Focus:** Retention rate, expansion revenue, CSM productivity, forecasting accuracy  
**Tools used:** Gainsight dashboards, Salesforce reports, Slack, executive reporting

**Goals:**
- Trust his churn forecast — currently margin of error is ±15%
- Understand which CSMs are managing account risks well vs. reactively
- Show CS contribution to expansion ARR
- Reduce the time his team spends on non-customer-facing work

**Pain points:**
- Health data in his dashboards is whatever CSMs last entered — often stale
- Churn surprises derail his quarterly forecasting
- Cannot easily identify which risks are being actively managed vs. ignored
- QBR quality is inconsistent across his team

**JTBD:**
- Show me the real state of my book of business, not what CSMs last entered
- Flag which accounts need my attention vs. are being managed well
- Help my team be more consistent and productive

---

### Persona 3: Priya — Account Manager (Expansion Focus)

**Role:** Account Manager responsible for expansion revenue in a shared-ownership CS model  
**Accounts:** 45 accounts, expansion target $1.8M ARR  
**Tools:** Salesforce, Gong, LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Goals:**
- Find expansion signals before they go cold
- Understand which product updates are most relevant to upsell conversations
- Prioritize which accounts are ready for expansion conversation vs. still in value realization

**Pain points:**
- No systematic way to know when a customer signals expansion intent
- Feature-to-account mapping is manual — she asks the product team which customers would benefit
- Cannot distinguish expansion-ready accounts from accounts that need more adoption work

**JTBD:**
- Tell me which accounts are ready to buy more and why
- Match product updates to the accounts where I should use them in conversations

---

### Persona 4: Dev — VP of Revenue Operations

**Role:** RevOps leader responsible for forecasting, tooling, and process excellence  
**Focus:** Forecast accuracy, CS tooling ROI, data integrity, cross-functional alignment

**Goals:**
- Reliable retention forecast for board reporting
- CS tooling stack that produces clean, analyzable data
- Connection between CS activity and revenue outcomes

**Pain points:**
- CS data quality is inconsistent — subjective health scores, incomplete notes
- Cannot attribute revenue retention to specific CS activities
- CS tools generate reports; not decision-grade intelligence

**JTBD:**
- Give me CS data I can use in forecast models
- Show me that CS activity is correlated to retention outcomes
